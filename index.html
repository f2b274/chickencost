Stop the Leak: The 20% Feed Trap

Feed is your largest ongoing expense. If your calculator is showing a loss, waste is usually the number one culprit.

The "Scratch & Spill" Effect: Chickens have a natural instinct to scratch. When using standard open-trough feeders or pans, they "bill out" the feed, sweeping it onto the ground where it mixes with bedding and feces. Studies show that a flock can waste up to 20% of their feed this way. You aren't just losing money; you're attracting rodents.

The Fix: Gravity-Port Feeders

By forcing the chicken to stick their head into a PVC or enclosed port, the "billing" motion is stopped completely. Any dropped feed falls back inside the container, not on the floor. This single upgrade often turns a monthly loss into a break-even instantly.

Max Yield: The Photoperiod Hack

Your foundation is solid. To scale your profit, we need to focus on maximizing lay frequency through biology, not just more feed.

The 14-Hour Rule: A hen's egg production is triggered by light entering her pineal gland. During the winter months, when daylight drops below 14 hours, a chicken's body naturally halts production to conserve energy. You can override this drop by providing supplemental morning light.

The Fix: Automated Coop Lighting

Never leave a light on 24/7—it stresses the birds and ruins their circadian rhythm. Instead, use a simple automated solar or battery-timed light that clicks on at 4:00 AM, giving them the 14 total hours of light they need to keep laying all winter long without running expensive high-voltage lines.
